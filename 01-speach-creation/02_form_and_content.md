### Speaking and writing are different mediums.

A lot of public speaking problems emerge when people treat writing as speech.

The audience is not going to read the speach, they will listen to it.

_______________________________________________________________________________

### Things that make speaking different from writing

1. Language is acquired, writing is learned.

Aquiring speach is a matter of listening, modelling, 
hypothesizing and testing.

2. Language evolved, writing was invented.

3. Language is ubiquitous, writing is not.
The majority of language have no writing systems.

_______________________________________________________________________________

You can't simply write an essay then read it out loud.

The visual techniques of writing don't automatically translate to speach.

Long sentences in writing, don't translate well when you have to deliver them
in spoken form.

Speach contains more `hedging devices` (E.g. "sort of", "kind of"),
and `intensifers` (E.g. "very", "really").

In writing these would be `junk words`, 
but in a speach they provide `important cues` for the listener.

`pitch variation` is also essential to good speaking.

When you try to `deliver` a speach that was designed as an writen essay, 
you are going to sound monotone and unatural.

Interestingly... `voice acting` or `acting` is about performing a written
script. I.e. Taking writen words and making them sound more natural.

Ignoring the differences between speaking and writing is like handing an
actor a bad script, 
even the best actor will struggle to make it sound natural and convincing.
