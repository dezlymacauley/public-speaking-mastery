Key point speach

This is a basic speech where you determine the key points that you want the
audience to remember.

Discuss each point with concreate and interesting examples.

Then deliver the speech clearly and with confidence.

Focus on how you audience wants to hear and process information in chunks

This speach is 3 to 7 minutes long (but it can be 
scaled to longer presentations)

A speech can be modularized into `7 to 8` interrelated key point speeches.

_______________________________________________________________________________

What to talk about?

Keep the topics general. Assume the audience has no background in the topic.
Non-controversional topics.

1. Invention = What does the audience need to hear to understand the topic?
The contents of the speach.

Did you explain the keypoints well?
Did you include relevant keypoints?
Any irrelevant keypoints?

2. Arragement = Putting the points in the most effective order.
Do you points transition well into each other?
Do the moments leading towards the ending give the audience cues that you
are about to end the speach.

3. Style = Phrase things concisely
Where the points too long or confusing?
Did you get into the details of the example quickly 
or were you dragging / rambling?

4. Memory = Remembering what to say
- Don't rely too much on your notes.
- You don't want to be reading. Eye contact with the audience.
- Do you give of the impression that you know what to say?

5. Delivery = Speaking clearly and confidently.
- Do you appear confident and animated?
- Breath work (Don't forget to breath between sentences) / 
Enunciation / Projection?
- Pitch and rate
- How is your movement and gesturing?
- Are movements distracting or well-timed?
- Was your speach within the time frame? (This requires you to pay attention
to the which topics should be included or excludes,
as well as practicing the speach so that it is executed smoothly)
