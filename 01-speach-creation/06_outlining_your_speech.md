### Structure / Example of organizing someone else speech

1. Intro
- Introduces the topic. E.g. In struggling economic times,
Universities try to save money by cutting departments that have few student
majors.

2. Makes a claim
- We should not enact this policy for two main reasons.

3. Support 1 (reason 1)
- It is harmful for students. If a student has a unique passion / career goal
E.g. dance, then that student can no longer persue that goal.
- It does not cater to post university goals. E.g. If the student wants a
unique skill on their resume that would make them stand out in the job market.

4. Support 2 (reason 2)
- It doesn't garantee savings.
- Students who want to enroll in these departments that are no longer there,
may decide to enroll in another school to fulfil their needs.
- This decrease in enrollment rates would affect the university negatively.
- The students who decide to stay would have to join a larger faculty,
which would increase class sizes an lead to Universities having to hire more
professors and teaching assistants.

5. Conclusion (Briefly repeat the claim and the conclusion of Support 1 and 2)
- Cutting these small departments and small majors, 
isn't a beneficial option for anyone. 
Firstly because it is harmful for students,
and secondly because it does not garantee savings.
