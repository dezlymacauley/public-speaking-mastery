### How to make it easier for the listener to decode the speach

`contextual clues`

What am I talking about? Why? and What I will be talking about next.

1. Don't sound monotone = This makes the listener have to work harder to stay
engaged as their brain scanes for contextual clues in what you are saying.

2. Don't speak too fast = This is cognitive load for the listener.
They are being bombared by one sentence before they have processed the next.

3. Long convuluted mumbling = The listener has to sort out the meaning in the
words.

When listening, you move back and forth between perceiving,

`prosodic cues`

prosody is all the sound and timing techniques when speaking.

Pitch, pausing, loudness

Decoding speach = Thinking about how the audience will hear and understand
the speach.

The listener needs cues to decode the speaking.
